## Objective

Today's goal was to understand the differences between Claude's models (Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus) and learn when to use each one. I also explored how different reasoning effort levels (Low, Standard, High, and Max) affect the quality and depth of Claude's responses.

---

## What I Did Today

I used Claude's personalized usage strategy prompt. Instead of giving generic recommendations, Claude first analyzed my profile, learning goals, projects, and daily workflow. Based on this information, it recommended the best model and reasoning effort for different types of tasks.

The generated strategy was tailored to my goal of becoming an AI Engineer and building a strong portfolio over the next year.

---

## What are Claude Models?

Claude provides different AI models designed for different types of work.

* **Haiku** is the fastest model and is best for quick questions, summaries, and simple tasks.
* **Sonnet** offers the best balance between speed, intelligence, and cost. It is suitable for coding, learning, and most daily work.
* **Opus** is designed for deep reasoning, complex planning, research, and advanced problem-solving where maximum reasoning quality is required.

---

## Difference Between Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus

| Model      | Best For                                                                                        |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Haiku**  | Quick answers, summaries, simple explanations, and small tasks.                                 |
| **Sonnet** | Coding, learning, debugging, AI projects, content creation, and daily productivity.             |
| **Opus**   | Research, architecture design, business strategy, interview preparation, and complex reasoning. |

---

## What Do Reasoning Effort Levels Mean?

Claude also allows different reasoning effort levels depending on how much thinking a task requires.

* **Low** – Fast responses for simple questions and quick tasks.
* **Standard** – Balanced reasoning suitable for most everyday work.
* **High** – More detailed reasoning for debugging, architecture decisions, and difficult technical problems.
* **Max** – Deepest level of reasoning for major projects, strategic planning, and important deliverables.

## My Personalized Claude Strategy

After analyzing my profile, Claude recommended **Sonnet** as my primary model because it provides the best balance between intelligence, speed, and efficiency for my daily work.

For most of my coding, AI learning, project development, and portfolio work, **Sonnet with Standard reasoning** is the best choice.

Claude suggested switching to **High** or **Max** reasoning only when working on important portfolio projects, complex debugging, research, or interview preparation.

Haiku should be used only for quick questions and simple tasks, while Opus is best reserved for deep research and major architectural decisions.

## Biggest Insight

The biggest thing I learned today is that **using the most powerful model for every task is not always the best approach**.

Choosing the right Claude model and reasoning effort based on the complexity of the task saves time while still producing high-quality results.

---

## What I Learned

Today's activity helped me understand that different Claude models serve different purposes.

I learned that:

* Sonnet is the best choice for most of my daily work.
* Haiku is useful when I need fast answers.
* Opus should be reserved for complex reasoning and strategic tasks.
* Standard effort is sufficient for most coding and learning tasks.
* High and Max effort should only be used when the quality of reasoning is more important than speed.
