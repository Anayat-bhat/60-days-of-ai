---

## What is Supply Chain Management?

Supply chain management is the process of coordinating the flow of materials, products, information, and resources from suppliers to manufacturers and ultimately to customers.

It includes activities such as:

- Procurement
- Supplier management
- Manufacturing
- Inventory management
- Warehousing
- Transportation
- Logistics
- Demand forecasting
- Risk management
- Customer delivery

Effective supply chain management ensures that the right products reach the right customers at the right time while controlling costs and maintaining operational efficiency.

---

## What is a Supply Chain Crisis?

A supply chain crisis is an unexpected event that disrupts the normal movement of materials, products, or services across a company's supply network.

Examples include:

- Factory fires
- Supplier bankruptcy
- Port strikes
- Cyberattacks
- Floods
- Raw material shortages
- Political conflicts
- Shipping delays

These disruptions can affect production, inventory availability, delivery times, profitability, and customer satisfaction.

---

## Why Supply Chain Resilience is Important

Supply chain resilience is the ability of an organization to prepare for disruptions, respond effectively when they occur, and recover while maintaining critical business operations.

A resilient supply chain may use strategies such as:

- Multiple suppliers
- Geographic supplier diversification
- Backup production capacity
- Safety stock
- Supplier risk monitoring
- Flexible logistics
- Crisis communication
- AI-powered forecasting and monitoring

The simulation showed me that companies should prepare for disruptions before they happen rather than depending entirely on reactive decisions.

# War Room

During the War Room stage, I had to select exactly three crisis response actions from six available strategies.

## Three War Room Actions Selected

1. **Expedite Shipments via Air Freight** (Tags: Fast, Expensive)
2. **Activate a Backup Supplier** (Tags: Resilience, Risk Reduction)
3. **Launch Proactive Customer Communication** (Tags: Trust-Building, Low Cost)

Each decision involved trade-offs. Some actions could improve delivery speed or customer satisfaction while increasing costs, while others could protect profitability but create additional operational or customer risks.

Customer Satisfaction scored an exceptional **97/100** because transparent customer communication paired with maintained delivery speeds preserved buyer trust and prevented order cancellations.

The War Room was one of the most interesting parts of the simulation because there was no perfect solution. Every decision involved balancing multiple business priorities.

# Supplier Negotiation Experience

The next stage involved negotiating with a supplier through four rounds.

The simulation tracked three important factors:

- Supplier Trust
- Price Pressure
- Lead Time Risk

My decisions in earlier negotiation rounds influenced the final outcome, demonstrating how supplier relationships develop over time.

## Four Negotiation Rounds

### Round 1 – Price Increase

The supplier proposed a 15% immediate price increase because of rising raw material costs.

**My Decision:** Accept the increase to preserve the relationship *(Built trust early, but accepted higher price baseline)*

### Round 2 – Minimum Order Quantity

The supplier asked for a commitment on minimum order quantities to reserve production capacity.

**My Decision:** Offer a moderate commitment tied to performance guarantees *(Aligned incentives and protected capacity with performance SLAs)*

### Round 3 – Faster Lead Times

The supplier offered guaranteed faster lead times for an additional premium.

**My Decision:** Pay the premium for guaranteed faster lead times *(Prioritized speed to protect delivery promises during the crisis)*

### Round 4 – Long-Term Agreement

The supplier requested a 12-month agreement to finalize the negotiation.

**My Decision:** Request a written service-level agreement (SLA) before signing *(Formalized expectations and secured recourse for potential performance drops)*

## Negotiation Score

**Negotiation Score: 38/100**

My biggest learning from this section was that successful supplier negotiation is not only about getting the lowest price. Trust, reliability, lead time, flexibility, and long-term relationships are also important.

# AI Strategy

After managing the immediate crisis, I had to choose two AI technologies that could improve the company's future supply chain.

The available options included:

- Demand Forecasting AI
- Inventory Optimization AI
- Supplier Risk Monitoring AI
- Warehouse Computer Vision
- Procurement Copilot

## Two AI Investments Selected

1. **Supplier Risk Monitoring AI**
2. **Demand Forecasting AI**

## Why I Selected These AI Investments

I selected these technologies because the crisis exposed severe supplier concentration vulnerability (a key supplier bankruptcy) and lead-time volatility. **Supplier Risk Monitoring AI** provides continuous monitoring of vendor financial health and news signals to flag distress before bankruptcy strikes, while **Demand Forecasting AI** uses machine learning to accurately predict customer demand and avoid costly inventory stockouts.

## Expected AI Business Impact

**Supplier Risk Monitoring AI:** Provides early warning of supplier distress — the single highest-leverage tool for preventing the next bankruptcy-style crisis.

**Demand Forecasting AI:** Reduces stockouts and overstock simultaneously by improving forecast accuracy — typically a 20-30% error reduction versus manual forecasting.

The AI Strategy section demonstrated how AI can support supply chain operations through better forecasting, inventory optimization, supplier risk detection, warehouse monitoring, and procurement assistance.
