## What Is a Digital Footprint?

A digital footprint is the trail of information created when a person uses websites, applications, social media platforms, online shopping services, search engines, payment applications, and other digital services.

A digital footprint can include information directly provided by a user as well as data generated through interactions with digital platforms.

Examples may include:
<!-- 
- Account information
- Search activity
- Social media interactions
- Device information
- App usage patterns
- Shopping activity
- Entertainment preferences
- Communication metadata
- Location-related information, depending on permissions and service behavior -->

The larger and more diverse a person's digital ecosystem becomes, the more important it is to understand and manage their privacy settings.

---

## What Does Digital Privacy Mean?

Digital privacy refers to the ability to understand and manage how personal information is collected, processed, stored, and potentially shared when using digital services.

Good digital privacy practices include reviewing application permissions, using strong authentication, limiting unnecessary data sharing, checking privacy settings, and understanding what information different online services may collect.

Digital privacy does not necessarily mean avoiding digital platforms. Instead, it means using them with greater awareness and making informed decisions about personal information.

## What Is Privacy Risk Assessment?

Privacy risk assessment is the process of identifying and evaluating potential privacy risks associated with digital services and online activities.

In this activity, Claude used the provided list of applications to estimate the overall digital exposure and identify areas that may represent higher privacy or tracking risks.
