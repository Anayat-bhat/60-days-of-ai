Day 8 – Claude Artifacts: Personal Environmental Health Analyzer

## Objective

Today's goal was to explore **Claude Artifacts** and understand how they can generate complete interactive applications instead of simple text responses. I built an Environmental Health Analyzer that uses real environmental data to provide health insights through an interactive dashboard.

---

## What I Did Today

I used the provided prompt to create a **Personal Environmental Health Analyzer** using Claude Artifacts. Claude automatically collected recent environmental data, analyzed it, and generated a complete interactive dashboard with charts, filters, report cards, and personalized recommendations.

Instead of receiving only text, I received a fully functional HTML application that could be opened in a web browser and interacted with like a real dashboard.

---

## What are Claude Artifacts?

Claude Artifacts are interactive applications that Claude can generate directly from prompts. Rather than producing only text, Artifacts can create complete HTML applications with dashboards, charts, filters, tables, cards, and responsive user interfaces.

They allow users to build working tools, reports, calculators, and visual applications without manually writing the code.

---

## What the Environmental Health Analyzer Does

The Environmental Health Analyzer uses environmental data to evaluate air quality and water quality for different cities.

The dashboard provides:

* Air Quality Index (AQI) analysis
* PM2.5 and PM10 pollution levels
* Environmental Health Score
* Water Quality Score
* Air and water quality grades
* Health risk analysis
* Personalized recommendations
* City comparison
* Interactive visualizations

For the selected city, it also explains how environmental conditions may affect lungs, sleep, exercise, skin health, and hair health.
