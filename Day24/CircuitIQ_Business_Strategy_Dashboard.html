SECTION 1

Startup Summary

  • Circuit.IQ is an AI-personalized virtual physics lab built by a solo student founder, targeting the gap between free-but-generic tools (OLabs, PhET) and expensive foreign platforms (Labster).
  • MVP is technically real and shipped: 100+ concurrent simulations at sub-second latency, built on React/TypeScript/Python/FastAPI.
  • Target market is India's private CBSE/ICSE schools (Classes 9–12) and Tier 2/3 engineering colleges — 300–2,000 students, digital-budget-aware, buyer is a Principal or HOD.
  • TAM is estimated at $150M with a 3-year SAM of $22M; founder-market fit is rated 6.1/10.
  • Zero customer validation to date: no interviews, no pilots, no revenue, no signed institution.
  • Core buying trigger is NEP 2020 compliance pressure plus poor practical exam outcomes and lab-equipment scarcity.
  • Pricing hypothesis is a per-institution annual subscription anchored near ₹25,000 (~$300), positioned between free government tools and premium foreign platforms.
  • The blueprint's own recommendation is CONDITIONAL GO: the 30–60 day mandate is customer proof, not more product — 8-12 interviews, one signed pilot, one willingness-to-pay signal.
  • Biggest structural risks: long institutional sales cycles (2-6 months) against solo-founder bandwidth, and a free government alternative (OLabs) that can undercut willingness to pay entirely.

SECTION 3

Business Reality Check

Who pays?

The institution (school Principal or college HOD/Dean), not the student and not the teacher. This is a B2B2C model where the economic buyer and the end user are different people — a structurally harder sale than direct-to-consumer, because it requires convincing an administrator who never touches the product day-to-day.

Why do they pay?

Only if practical-score improvement or NEP 2020 compliance can be demonstrated in a way that justifies budget against a free alternative (OLabs) that already exists. Pain is real (severity 80/100 per the blueprint), but pain alone does not create willingness to pay when a free substitute is "good enough" for compliance box-ticking.

How will they discover the product?

Currently: LinkedIn content (ABTalks audience), founder's personal network, and cold outreach — all founder-bottlenecked, zero-budget channels with no proof of institutional conversion yet.

Biggest growth risk

Sales-cycle length (2-6 months) versus solo-founder bandwidth. A single founder cannot run 30-40 parallel institutional sales motions while also building product. Growth is capped by founder hours, not demand.

Biggest monetization risk

OLabs is free, government-backed, and "good enough" for compliance optics. If institutions cannot articulate a concrete outcome (score lift, accreditation point) that Circuit.IQ delivers and OLabs doesn't, price resistance will be near-total.

Weakest assumptions to validate first

(1) That any institution will pay real money rather than only accept a free pilot forever. (2) That ₹25,000/institution/year is anchored to a real budget line rather than founder guesswork. (3) That founder-led LinkedIn/network outreach can scale past the first 2-3 warm-network wins.

SECTION 4

Business Strategy Report

4.1 Executive Summary

Circuit.IQ has real technical substance and a structurally validated problem in Indian physics education — but it is currently a product in search of a business model, not a business. The founder has built a genuinely capable MVP (sub-second simulation latency at 100+ concurrent users) while skipping the harder, non-technical work: talking to buyers. With zero interviews, zero pilots, and zero revenue, every commercial assumption in the blueprint — including the ₹25,000 price anchor — is a guess dressed as a hypothesis. The single highest-leverage action for the next 30–60 days is not more code; it is 8-12 structured conversations with principals and HODs that either produce a pilot commitment or kill the current pricing/packaging assumption before more time is sunk into it.

4.3 Revenue & Pricing Strategy

The blueprint's ₹25,000 (~$300) per-institution annual anchor is reasonable as a starting hypothesis but is not yet evidence-based. Recommended approach: treat pricing as a variable to be discovered during interviews, not a fixed number to defend.

TierStructurePurpose
PilotFree, 4-8 weeks, 1-2 classes. No card, no PO required.Remove all friction to first usage; generate the first outcome data point.
Core Annual LicensePer-institution (not per-student) flat fee, anchored near ₹20,000-30,000/yr depending on class-section count.Primary revenue line; matches how Indian institutions actually procure software.
Analytics / Accreditation Add-onOptional premium tier for colleges needing NBA/NAAC-style accreditation reporting.Higher-margin upsell for the segment with the strongest budget authority (HOD/Dean).

Per-institution (flat) pricing is the correct call over per-student: it removes headcount-audit friction from procurement and matches how school budgets are actually approved.

4.5 Customer Acquisition Strategy

  • Lead with outcomes, not features: every outreach message should reference the score/compliance pain, not the AI technology.
  • Use warm-network-first sequencing: alumni, professors, and family/community contacts in J&K before cold LinkedIn outreach — institutional buyers trust referred vendors far more than cold ones.
  • Convert ABTalks content into a lead magnet: a short "NEP 2020 practical-compliance checklist" post that funnels principals to a demo link, rather than generic AI-education content.
  • Track everything from interview #1: objections, willingness-to-pay language, and budget-cycle timing — this becomes the case study and the sales script simultaneously.
  • Do not build paid acquisition into the plan yet — CAC only makes sense once one channel (referral) is proven to convert at all.

4.7 Competitive Position & Moat

CompetitorPosition vs. Circuit.IQ
OLabs / PhET (free, govt-backed)Biggest real threat — "good enough" for compliance, zero cost. Circuit.IQ must win on demonstrated outcome lift and UX, not on existence of AI.
Labster (premium, foreign)Priced out of the target segment ($79-109/student/yr vs. Indian institutional budgets); Circuit.IQ's whitespace is directly below this price point.
Generic AI-tutoring startupsNot physics-lab-specific; Circuit.IQ's simulation depth is a defensible niche if maintained.

Current moat is weak: technical execution (sub-second simulation performance) is real but replicable, and the founder has no distribution moat, no brand trust, and no data moat yet. The only durable moat achievable at this stage is trust and outcome proof — a published case study and word-of-mouth within the tight-knit Indian school-principal network is worth more right now than any additional feature.

SECTION 7

Founder Action Sheet — Top 10 Actions

  1. Write a 10-question buyer-interview script targeting practical-score pain, budget cycle, and OLabs comparison.
  2. Build a target list of 30-40 schools/colleges, starting in Srinagar/J&K where the founder has network access.
  3. Reach out via LinkedIn (leveraging the ABTalks audience), email, and personal/alumni network — warm contacts first.
  4. Conduct 8-12 structured interviews within 14 days; log every objection and every willingness-to-pay signal verbatim.
  5. Identify the 2-3 most promising institutions based on interview signal, not just accessibility.
  6. Design a free, 4-8 week, 1-2 class pilot offer with clearly defined, pre-agreed success metrics.
  7. Secure at least one signed or clearly verbal pilot commitment before writing any more product code.
  8. Build and test a genuinely low-bandwidth demo mode on real, low-end school devices — not just on the founder's laptop.
  9. Instrument pilot usage tracking plus a simple pre/post concept quiz to generate the first outcome data point.
  10. Publish the pilot outcome as a short case study and use it to unlock the next institution and the first paid conversation.
SECTION 8

Sustainability Verdict

Circuit.IQ is not yet a sustainable business — it is a well-built product with a plausible but entirely unproven revenue model, sitting at Composite Score 42/100 (VALIDATE). Sustainability hinges on three unresolved questions: whether any institution will pay real money against a free substitute, whether the ₹25,000 price anchor survives contact with a real budget conversation, and whether founder-led distribution can produce more than 2-3 warm-network wins. The next 60 days — 8-12 interviews, one signed pilot, one written willingness-to-pay signal — will answer all three; until then, capital, headcount, and further feature-building should stay paused.
