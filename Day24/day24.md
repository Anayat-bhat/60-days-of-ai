# Day 24 – Customer Discovery Instruments & Outreach Campaign

## Objective

The objective of Day 24 was to design the practical instruments and outreach materials required to execute the customer discovery plan for **Circuit.IQ – AI-Powered Virtual Physics Lab** formulated on Day 23. This involved developing structured interview scripts for key educational stakeholders, writing cold outreach templates for emails and LinkedIn, and creating a feedback log schema to organize and analyze qualitative responses.

---

## The Stakeholder Interview Scripts

To ensure customer discovery interviews yield actionable insights rather than polite encouragement, the questions are designed to be open-ended, focused on past and present behaviors, and specifically tailored to the unique pain points of each stakeholder.

### 1. Primary Decision Maker: School Principals / Directors / College Deans
*Focus: Budget, compliance (NEP 2020), procurement cycles, institutional reputation, and student outcomes.*

| # | Question | Core Objective / Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | "How are you currently adapting your practical science curriculum to align with the new NEP 2020 experiential learning guidelines?" | Explores compliance pressure and identifies if NEP is an active buying trigger. |
| **2** | "Can you tell me about the last time you approved a digital learning tool or lab resource for your science department? What was that process like?" | Maps out the procurement flow, budget windows, and decision-making timeline. |
| **3** | "What is the biggest operational challenge your science departments face when managing physical labs and equipment?" | Pinpoints issues like equipment breakage, high maintenance costs, or scheduling constraints. |
| **4** | "How does the performance of students in practical examinations affect the school’s overall academic reputation or admissions?" | Connects the solution to high-stakes institutional outcomes (reputation, admissions). |
| **5** | "If a teacher approaches you requesting a new digital teaching assistant or simulation tool, what criteria do you use to evaluate whether to approve it?" | Identifies key buying obstacles and decision criteria (e.g., cost, proof of concept, teacher load). |
| **6** | "How much does your school currently spend annually per student on maintaining physical labs and consumable materials?" | Establishes a financial baseline to evaluate our pricing hypothesis (₹25,000/year). |
| **7** | "What is your institution's policy regarding the onboarding of new external software? What security, privacy, or compliance checks must be passed?" | Evaluates technical risks and compliance hurdles regarding child/student data. |
| **8** | "Can you recall a software tool that your staff stopped using after you paid for it? Why do you think that happened?" | Highlights potential retention/onboarding bottlenecks to avoid in our product. |
| **9** | "How open is your school to running a structured, 4-week pilot program for a virtual lab tool before any commercial discussions take place?" | Validates our proposed low-friction entry strategy (pilot-first approach). |
| **10** | "Who else in your institution, such as HODs or administrative trustees, would need to review a platform like this before a pilot is signed off?" | Map secondary decision-makers and influencers within the target organization. |

---

### 2. Primary Influencer: Physics Teachers & HODs (Head of Departments)
*Focus: Syllabus mapping, teaching load, lesson planning, student engagement, and software ease-of-use.*

| # | Question | Core Objective / Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | "What physics concepts or experiments do your students struggle with the most in the physical lab? Why is that?" | Identifies target simulations to prioritize (e.g., electromagnetism, optics, wave mechanics). |
| **2** | "How do you prepare students for a physical lab session before they actually touch the equipment? What resources do they use?" | Evaluates if prep-work is a pain point and where digital pre-lab runs fit. |
| **3** | "Walk me through how you grade and assess a student's performance in a practical class. How long does it take you?" | Looks for opportunities to automate grading/assessments with our teacher dashboard. |
| **4** | "What is your experience with free tools like OLabs or PhET? What do you find most useful, and what is missing?" | Pinpoints competitor weaknesses (e.g., lack of analytics, syllabus misalignment, poor UX). |
| **5** | "How do you track which individual students are falling behind in understanding practical concepts during a large class?" | Validates demand for student misconception analytics and performance dashboards. |
| **6** | "How much time do you spend setting up, calibrating, and putting away lab apparatus for a single class session?" | Measures the time-saving value proposition of virtual simulation pre-runs. |
| **7** | "What kind of training or support would you need to confidently run a class using a digital virtual physics lab tool?" | Identifies onboarding friction points and training documentation requirements. |
| **8** | "How do you handle situations where a student misses a lab class due to absence? How do they make up for it?" | Explores remote learning use cases and asynchronous lab access opportunities. |
| **9** | "What bandwidth and device hardware constraints exist in your school's computer lab or on students' home devices?" | Validates the engineering requirements for our proposed "Low-Bandwidth Demo Mode." |
| **10** | "What reports or dashboards would be most helpful for you to present to the principal to prove your students' progress?" | Identifies specific export formats (PDF, Excel, WhatsApp summaries) required. |

### 3. End User: Class 11 & 12 Science Students
*Focus: UI/UX satisfaction, self-paced learning, conceptual clarity, and homework load.*

| # | Question | Core Objective / Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | "What is the hardest part about doing physics experiments in your school lab?" | Highlights student-centric frustrations (e.g., rushed sessions, confusing manuals, broken parts). |
| **2** | "How confident do you feel when you write your practical lab records? Where do you look for help?" | Explores student self-study resources and potential content gaps. |
| **3** | "When you are studying for a physics test, how do you visualize things you can't see, like electric current or magnetic field lines?" | Validates the need for real-time visualization features in Circuit.IQ simulations. |
| **4** | "If you make a mistake in a school lab experiment, what happens? How do you fix it?" | Tests the psychological safety of a risk-free virtual laboratory environment. |
| **5** | "Have you ever used OLabs, PhET, or YouTube videos to study for your practicals? What did you like or dislike about them?" | Gathers direct UX feedback on competitors from the student perspective. |
| **6** | "How much time do you spend studying physics at home, and what device (phone, laptop, family PC) do you use most?" | Informs the device compatibility strategy (mobile responsiveness vs. desktop focus). |
| **7** | "If you had access to a web-based lab simulator that lets you build circuits or run tests at home, when would you use it?" | Explores usage frequency, homework vs. exam preparation triggers. |
| **8** | "What makes a learning app or website engaging for you? What makes you close it immediately?" | Identifies gamification preferences, UI style trends, and UX detractor triggers. |
| **9** | "How do you feel when you receive instant automated hints while solving a problem versus having to ask a teacher?" | Validates the utility of the AI-powered adaptive guide feature. |
| **10** | "If this virtual lab cost a small amount per month for personal study, would you ask your parents to pay for it, or would you expect your school to provide it?" | Tests consumer/parent monetization options versus institutional licensing. |

## Customer Discovery Feedback Log Schema

To prevent qualitative feedback from getting lost in emails or notepad entries, we will organize all interview outputs in a structured feedback database.

### Database Schema Definition

| Field Name | Data Type | Description | Example Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| `interview_id` | String (UUID) | Unique identifier for each customer interview. | `CID-2026-001` |
| `date` | Date | Date when the interview was conducted. | `2026-07-17` |
| `contact_name` | String | Name of the interviewee. | `Ritu Sharma` |
| `contact_role` | String | Designation of the interviewee. | `School Principal` |
| `institution_name`| String | Name of the school or college. | `Delhi Public School, Srinagar` |
| `institution_type`| Enum | School (CBSE/ICSE) or College (Engineering/Polytechnic). | `School (CBSE)` |
| `demographics` | String | Location and estimated student count. | `Srinagar, J&K (800 students)` |
| `current_solution` | String | How they currently solve the problem. | `OLabs, physical labs only` |
| `core_pain_point` | Text | The most critical problem they voiced. | `Equipment maintenance is high; students lack lab time before board exams.` |
| `ne_compliance` | Boolean | Is NEP 2020 compliance an active priority? | `TRUE` |
| `primary_objection`| Text | The main reason they might reject the pilot/product.| `Teachers are resistant to learning new software.` |
| `wtp_signal` | Enum | Willingness-to-pay signal: High, Medium, Low, None. | `Medium (If board results show improvement)` |
| `follow_up_action`| Text | Immediate next step agreed upon. | `Send email demo link to HOD Physics on Monday.` |
