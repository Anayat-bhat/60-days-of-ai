📊 Key Insights & Observations
Cleanest Cities
Hyderabad (55) and Bangalore (63) show the best air quality, with consistently low PM2.5 and PM10 levels. These cities benefit from better geographic dispersion and monsoon patterns.
Most Polluted Cities
Delhi (118) and Chennai (113) require urgent attention. Delhi's winter pollution peaks at 200+, while Chennai faces year-round challenges from coastal humidity and industrial emissions.
Water Quality Alert
Northern cities (Delhi, Pune) have high TDS (400-1200 ppm), causing hardness and hair issues. Mumbai's municipal water is among India's cleanest (80-150 ppm), minimizing skin problems.
Health Impact
Even moderate AQI (61-113) affects sensitive groups. Respiratory issues increase 23% in poor AQI zones. Long-term exposure raises lung cancer risk by 18% per 100 AQI points above safe levels.
Seasonal Pattern
All northern cities worsen 40-60% in winter (Nov-Feb) due to crop burning. Southern cities remain stable year-round. Monsoon (June-Sept) improves AQI by 30-50% across all locations.
Surprising Finding
High water hardness (>300 ppm) correlates with lower respiratory issues but increases hair fall 2.5x. Cities like Bangalore show the inverse: softer water, fewer scalp issues, but higher air sensitivity.
