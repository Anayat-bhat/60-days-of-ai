# Day 6 – ATS Resume Optimization

## Objective

Today's goal was to understand how Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) evaluate resumes and learn how to improve my resume so that it is easier for both ATS software and recruiters to read.

---

## What I Did Today

I uploaded my existing resume to Claude and used an ATS Resume Optimizer prompt. Claude analyzed my resume, identified formatting and keyword issues, and generated an improved one-page ATS-friendly version.

It also explained why certain changes would improve the chances of my resume being parsed correctly by ATS systems and reviewed more effectively by recruiters.

---

## What is ATS?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software used by many companies to scan, organize, and filter resumes before they reach a recruiter. It looks for important information such as skills, education, experience, and keywords related to the job description.

If a resume has poor formatting or missing keywords, it may not be parsed correctly, reducing the chances of being shortlisted.

---

## Why ATS Optimization Matters

Optimizing a resume for ATS helps ensure that important information is correctly recognized by the software. It also improves readability for recruiters by using a clean layout, clear section headings, and relevant keywords.

A well-optimized resume increases the likelihood of passing the initial screening process for internships and job applications.

---

## ATS Score

**Previous Score:** 58/100

**Optimized Score:** 88/100

The optimization significantly improved both ATS compatibility and recruiter readability.

---

## Changes Claude Made

Claude suggested several practical improvements to my resume:

* Removed emojis from the contact section so ATS systems can correctly read my phone number and email.
* Replaced styled and colored section headings with simple uppercase headings that ATS software can recognize.
* Removed decorative formatting, borders, and visual elements that may interfere with ATS parsing.
* Rewrote bullet points using stronger action verbs to improve clarity and impact.
* Standardized my GitHub username throughout the resume to maintain consistency.
* Renamed **"Vector & Data"** to **"Databases & Vector Stores"** to improve keyword matching.
* Expanded my degree title to **Bachelor of Technology in Artificial Intelligence & Data Science** for better keyword recognition.
* Removed repetitive wording and improved the overall flow of the resume.

---

## What I Learned

Today's activity taught me that a good resume is not only about listing skills and projects but also about presenting them in a format that ATS systems can understand.

I also learned that:

* Simple formatting is better than decorative designs.
* Strong action verbs make achievements more impactful.
* Consistent information improves professionalism.
* Relevant keywords help ATS match resumes with job descriptions.
* Truthful and clear content is more valuable than adding unnecessary details.

---

## Biggest Observation

The biggest thing I noticed today is that **small formatting changes can have a significant impact on ATS compatibility**.

Removing emojis, using standard section headings, and improving keyword usage increased my estimated ATS score from **58/100 to 88/100** without changing the actual content of my resume.

This showed me that presentation is just as important as the information itself.
