## Section 1: What is Prompt Engineering?

**Prompt Engineering** is the skill of writing clear, specific instructions for an AI like Claude so it gives you the most useful response possible.

Think of it like giving directions. If you tell someone "go somewhere nice for lunch," they might pick a place you'd hate. But if you say "find a quiet Italian restaurant within 10 minutes of my office that has pasta under $15" — you're going to get exactly what you need.

A **prompt** is simply what you type to the AI. Prompt Engineering is the art of typing it *well*.

**Why does it matter?**
AI tools like Claude are powerful — but they respond based entirely on what you give them. A vague question gets a vague answer. A well-structured prompt gets a precise, useful, tailored response. Prompt Engineering is the difference between getting a generic reply and getting something you can actually use.

## Section 2: Weak vs. Improved Prompt Example

**Weak Prompt:**
> *"Write something about marketing."*

This is too vague. Claude doesn't know the audience, tone, length, goal, or format. The output will be generic.

**Improved Prompt:**
> *"Write a 3-paragraph LinkedIn post for small business owners explaining why email marketing outperforms social media ads in 2025. Use a friendly, conversational tone and end with a call-to-action encouraging readers to start their first email list."*

This tells Claude: **who** it's for, **what** to write, **how long**, **the tone**, **the topic**, and **what outcome you want**. The result? Something you can almost post immediately.

**Three practical benefits of writing better prompts:**

1. **Save time** — You get a usable response on the first try instead of going back and forth refining.
2. **Get better quality** — Specific prompts produce focused, relevant, and professional-grade output.
3. **Stay in control** — You shape the AI's response rather than hoping it guesses what you need.
